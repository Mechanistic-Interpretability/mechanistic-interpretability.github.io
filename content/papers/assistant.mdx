## Impact

The paper finds a single “Assistant Axis” in model activations that controls and stabilizes persona.

The article describes research showing that large language models implicitly represent many character archetypes in a shared “persona space.” By measuring internal activations for 275 roles (like editor, ghost, consultant), the authors find that the main direction of variation in this space corresponds to how Assistant‑like a persona is. They call this dominant direction the Assistant Axis: one end clusters helpful, professional roles (evaluator, consultant), while the opposite end clusters fantastical or un-assistant-like roles (ghost, hermit, leviathan). This axis already exists in base (pre‑trained) models and aligns with human helper archetypes like therapists and coaches, which post‑training then refines into the Assistant persona.
